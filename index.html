Jobs lost in MA in the pandemic

By Marina Villeneuve

How have Massachusetts' public and private sectors fared since the pandemic?

I looked at Bureau of Labor Statistics data to get some insight.

BLS data isn't perfect, and there are lots of different ways to gauge how well the economy is doing now compared to pre-pandemic.

But sector-specific employment data can provide crucial context about which parts of the economy are seeing growth or declines. And it could be useful to simply compare where MA jobs stand according to the latest data, so I'm looking at May 2023 to May 2019.

I found that the private sector as well as local government jobs have rebounded in MA. Both sectors saw a 1% increase in jobs from May 2019 to May 2023 -- with an increase of 43,400 private sector jobs and 1,300 local government jobs.

However, the state government sector has yet to regain all jobs lost in the pandemic.

State government -- which employs every from corrections guards to teaching assistants at state universities -- are only at 93% of pre-pandemic levels.

State government has seen a 7 % drop: meaning a loss of 9,100 state government jobs.

The state government education sector saw an 11% drop of 7,300 jobs -- this sector includes teaching assistants.

Other hard-hit sectors include state government hospitals, which lost 800 jobs for a 11% drop.

The drop in jobs at state government hospitals got me curious about how the hospital sector is faring overall.

The hospital sector overall rebounded in the pandemic...

but I found that nursing homes have yet to recover.
